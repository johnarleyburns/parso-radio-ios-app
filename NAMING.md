## ⚑ UPDATE (2026-05-28) — trademark search changes the pick → **Airloom**

Ran live USPTO trademark searches (RapidAPI). Result: **the dictionary-word
finalists are too crowded/conflicted to own cleanly; a coinage wins.**

| Name | Active marks | Software/media (cls 9/38/41/42) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Aether** | 181 | crowded — `AETHER GAZER` (cls 9+42), `AETHER GAMES`/`AETHERCLOUD`/`AETHERLAB` (42), `AETHERA` (9+42)… bare "Aether" only in HVAC/jewelry/retail | ⚠️ risky, crowded field, likely refusal/opposition for an app |
| **Spindle** | 49 | **worse** — a live **exact "SPINDLE"** mark for *downloadable + online software* (cls 9/42) | ❌ same-word/same-class conflict |
| **Airloom** | **3** | **none** — only HVAC, clothing, retail (all unrelated) | ✅ **clean for audio** |

**Recommendation: `Airloom`** — coinage of *heirloom* (timeless public-domain
treasures) + *air* (airwaves/radio). Trademark field is clean in our classes,
it's distinctive/registrable (a coined mark is inherently stronger), and the
concept fits the whole catalog. Caveats:
- **App Store name "Airloom" is taken** (a Photo/Video app + a Lifestyle app, both
  unrelated) → list as **"Airloom Radio"** (unique; the brand is still "Airloom").
- Domains: `getairloom.com` / `airloom.radio` looked free; `.app/.fm/.audio` taken.
- The two existing `AIRLOOM` registrations (HVAC, clothing) are unrelated classes,
  so an audio/entertainment "Airloom" should coexist — but still get a formal
  attorney clearance before filing.

_The original Aether-first analysis below predates the trademark data; keep it

## What the name must do (recap)
Re-frame off "Music" (the app is mostly spoken-word + classical), be findable
(distinctive enough to own), carry the free/open/timeless feel, and be
trademark-clear, pronounceable, and spellable.

## Live checks (2026-05-28)
- **App Store** (iTunes Search API): **none of the three has an exact-name app**
  — all are free to claim as an app name. No collision in the audio/music/radio
  space for any of them.
- **USPTO**: their search backend isn't scriptable from here — the trademark

- **Domains** (rough DNS signal, verify with whois): `aether.fm`,

## The finalists

### 1. Aether  ⭐ recommended
*The classical "fifth element" / the medium through which waves travel; also the
upper heavens.*
- **Fit (best):** "airwaves" → radio; "ethereal" → ambient; "the heavens /
  antiquity" → classical + public-domain + knowledge. The only finalist that
  comfortably covers *all* the content (books, lectures, classical, news,
  ambient), not just music.
- **Sound/spell:** says cleanly as "EE-ther"; the "Ae-" spelling is a touch of
  friction but is recognizable and reads as premium (and usefully separates it
  from crypto "Ether").
- **Distinctiveness:** evocative and ownable; pairs well as "Aether" alone or
  "Aether Radio" / "Aether FM" to anchor the category.
- **App Store:** no audio app; nearest is a game ("Aether Gazer") and health
  apps — different categories.
- **TM risk:** "Aether/Ether" appears in crypto and gaming, **not** in
  audio/music/radio — different class, but verify.
- **Watch-outs:** minor — "ether" also = anesthetic / "lost in the ether"; the
  poetic sense dominates.

### 2. Spindle  — strong runner-up (lean into the wheel)
*The rod a record/spool spins on; also evokes the iPod click-wheel spinning.*
- **Fit:** reinforces the app's most distinctive asset — the click-wheel UI —
  and the turntable/record nostalgia. Warm, tactile, concrete.
- **Sound/spell:** "SPIN-dl" — easy to say and spell.
- **Weakness:** skews **musical / records**, so it under-represents the
  audiobooks + lectures + news that are most of the catalog (the same trap as
  "Music"). Also a more generic everyday word (textile/HDD spindle) → less
  premium than Aether.
- **App Store:** only a puzzle game + a doc scanner. **TM:** some dev tools
  ("Spindle" static-site/CMS) — different class; verify.

### 3. Audire  — clean but I'd avoid it
*Latin "to hear/listen" — the literal root of audio, audible, audience.*
- **Upside:** completely unclaimed on the App Store; coined, brandable; "listen"
  meaning fits everything.
- **The dealbreaker:** it's the root **Audible** derives from, and your app
  prominently does **audiobooks**. "Audire" next to Amazon's Audible invites
  user confusion *and* a plausible trademark challenge in the *same* category
  Audible dominates. High-risk for an audiobook-playing app.
- **Also:** ambiguous pronunciation ("ow-DEE-ray"? "AW-dyre"? "ah-DEER"?) hurts
  word-of-mouth.

## Recommendation: **Aether**

It's the only finalist that fits the *whole* content mix (its weakest rivals
each skew one way — Spindle to music, Audire to audiobooks-but-near-Audible),
it's distinctive and premium yet easy to say, it's free on the App Store, and
its sole real risk (out-of-class crypto/gaming TM) is the most manageable of the
three. Lead with **Aether**; use the subtitle to anchor the category.

- **App name:** `Aether` (or `Aether Radio` if you want the category in the name)
- **Subtitle (≤30):** `Free classical, books & radio` (28) — or
  `Public-domain audio, free` (25)
- **Taglines:** "The free airwaves of human knowledge." · "Timeless audio. No
  ads, no fees."

**If you'd rather double down on the click-wheel identity over breadth → Spindle**
is the pick. **Audire → no**, for the Audible adjacency.

## Before committing
1. Formal trademark search (TESS/tmsearch or counsel), classes 9 & 41.
2. Confirm the App Store name is still free at submission (it can be claimed by
   anyone between now and then).
3. Grab a domain: `aetherradio.app` looked open; also check `getaether.app`,
   `aether.app`, `listenaether.com`. (You keep parso.guru regardless.)
4. Note: the App Store *listing name* and the bundle id (`guru.parso.ios-radio-app`)
   are independent — the bundle id can stay; only the display/listing name changes.
